Types of Ad Implants

1. Single Tooth implant Crown:

Removable dentures can be secured to implants using attachments, offering more stability than traditional dentures. The choice of implant prosthesis depends on missing teeth, oral health, bone density, and preferences, requiring professional consultation for the best option.

2. Implant-Supported Bridge:

When multiple adjacent teeth are missing, an implant-supported bridge can be used. Dental implants are placed on either side of the gap,and a bridge consisting of multiple connected crowns is attached to the implants,effectively filling the space.

3. All-on-4 or All-on-6 ADimplant prosthesis:

Implant-supported prostheses like All-on-4 or All-on-6 use four or six implants to support a full arch of teeth. They reduce cost and complexity, and come in types like hybrid dentures, metal-ceramic, Emax, or zirconia.

4. Implant-Supported Overdentures:

Why To Choose Ad Implant

1. Biocompatibility

AD-implant materials are biocompatible, meaning they don’t cause adverse reactions in the body. Common materials include titanium, zirconia, and ceramic.

6. Minimal Maintenance

ADimplant prostheses require minimal maintenance, avoiding staining, chipping, or deterioration under normal use.

2. Durability

AD-implant prostheses are made from durable materials to withstand chewing and biting forces over time, ensuring long-lasting performance.

7. Gum Tissue Compatibility

AD-implant prostheses are designed to fit naturally with gum tissues, promoting healthy gums and a natural smile.

3. Natural Appearance

AD-implant prostheses closely resemble natural teeth in color, shape, and texture, enhancing aesthetics and confidence.

8. Proper Occlusion

AD-implant prostheses ensure proper bite alignment, preventing jaw pain and uneven wear on teeth.

4. Stability and Fit

ADimplant prostheses fit securely, ensuring comfort and preventing slippage during speaking and eating for overall satisfaction.

9. Functional Efficiency

AD-implant prostheses restore efficient chewing and speaking, allowing comfort with a variety of foods and clear speech.

5. Ease of Cleaning

AD-implant prostheses are easy to clean, helping maintain oral hygiene and prevent plaque buildup and gum issues.

10. Customization

Each AD-implant prosthesis are custom-made to fit the patient's unique oral anatomy, ensurinf a pieacise and coomfortable fit.
