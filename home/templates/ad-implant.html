What is an AD-Implant Prosthesis ?

These finely crafted prosthetic teeth are custom-made to match the color, shape, and size of your natural teeth, ensuring a seamless and natural-looking smile. They are securely attached to dental implants, which act as artificial tooth roots, providing a strong and stable foundation for daily functions like chewing and speaking. The result is a restoration that not only looks natural but also feels and performs like real teeth.

AD-Imaplant prostheses are a popular and highly effective solution for replacing missing teeth. They help restore functionality, improve aesthetics, and promote better oral health by preventing bone loss and maintaining proper alignment of surrounding teeth. Whether you're missing a single tooth or multiple teeth, implant-supported prosthetics offer a durable, long-term solution that enhances both your confidence and quality of life.

Types of AD-Implants

{% comment %} {% endcomment %}

1. Single Tooth Implant Crown:

Removable dentures can be secured to implants using attachments, offering more stability than traditional dentures. The choice of implant prosthesis depends on missing teeth, oral health, bone density, and preferences, requiring professional consultation for the best option.

2. Implant-Supported Bridge:

When multiple adjacent teeth are missing, an implant-supported bridge can be used. Dental implants are placed on either side of the gap,and a bridge consisting of multiple connected crowns is attached to the implants,effectively filling the space.

3. All-on-4 or All-on-6 AD-Implant prosthesis:

Implant-supported prostheses like All-on-4 or All-on-6 use four or six implants to support a full arch of teeth. They reduce cost and complexity, and come in types like hybrid dentures, metal-ceramic, Emax, or zirconia.

4. Implant-Supported Overdentures:

Removable dentures can be secured to implants using attachments, offering more stability than traditional dentures. The choice of implant prosthesis depends on missing teeth, oral health, bone density, and preferences, requiring professional consultation for the best option.
